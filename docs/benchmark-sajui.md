# Saju-i Benchmark Notes For International Adaptation

## What Saju-i Does Well

Saju-i packages a complicated traditional system into small, approachable products:

- `990 won Saju` as the impulse entry product.
- Compatibility, decade luck, annual luck, daily fortune, auspicious date selection, and direct chat consultation as adjacent upsells.
- A very explicit promise: the reading is detailed, more generous than expected, and emotionally validating.
- The report is divided into scannable sections with strong titles.
- Technical chart data appears first, then the prose interpretation translates it into everyday life.
- The tone mixes traditional authority, direct feedback, affectionate encouragement, and social-media language.
- The bottom navigation mirrors a mobile app: home, add chart, chat, saved readings.

## What Should Change Overseas

International users may not know Saju, Korean lunar calendar culture, or terms like `Shinsal`, `Ten Gods`, `Luck Pillars`, and `Manse calendar`. The overseas version needs more explanation inside the product itself.

Use these adaptations:

- Lead with `Korean Four Pillars` and mention `similar to BaZi` for discoverability.
- Explain that birth date, birth time, birthplace, timezone, and solar-time correction change the chart.
- Keep Korean/Chinese terms available, but default the UI to plain English.
- Translate technical markers into short, poetic consumer labels.
- Add tooltips and glossary chips beside every technical term.
- Avoid deterministic claims. Use language like `tendency`, `theme`, `timing`, `pattern`, and `reflection`.
- Add a cultural note: Saju is a traditional interpretive system, not scientific, medical, legal, or financial advice.

## Content Formula

Each report should follow this sequence:

1. Snapshot title: a memorable metaphor for the chart.
2. Objective basis: what chart data created the interpretation.
3. Plain-language interpretation: personality, work, money, love, family, social life, location, timing.
4. Direct reality check: one section that is more blunt and practical.
5. Warm reinforcement: emotional closure that feels personal.
6. Lucky actions: colors, directions, habits, environment, and timing.

## Tone Rules

Use a modern, polished English tone:

- Warm but not childish.
- Direct but not cruel.
- Trend-aware but not meme-dependent.
- Specific enough to feel premium.
- Transparent about cultural context.

Good pattern:

> Your chart has a strong Water signature, so the reading starts with sensitivity, memory, and emotional depth. The issue is not that you feel too much; it is that you need warmer systems around you so the feeling can become motion.

Avoid:

> You are cursed by Water and must never invest.

## UX Implications

The overseas UI must teach while selling:

- Product cards should include a one-line promise and a `what you get` preview.
- The chart screen should show the Four Pillars, Hidden Stems, Ten Gods, Life Stages, Shinsal, and Relationships.
- Every technical chip should open a plain-English explanation.
- Users should be able to toggle `Simple`, `Technical`, and `Poetic` labels.
- The report should use accordions so a long reading feels intentional, not overwhelming.
